# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Friends who want the social tension and tactical depth of a classic resource-trading board game without accounts, setup work, or a visually flat digital table. They play primarily on desktop, either together on a call or around separate screens, and need every legal choice and hidden-information boundary to remain obvious.

## Product Purpose

Rill is an original, browser-based strategy game for three or four human and bot players. It makes room creation, joining, trading, building, reconnecting, and learning feel effortless while preserving the complete agreed base-game rules. Success means a new room is playable within a minute, experienced players trust the rules engine, and repeated sessions feel responsive rather than ceremonial.

## Brand Personality

Warm, tactile, alive. Rill should feel like a hand-illustrated map under evening lamplight: inviting enough for a first game, precise enough for competitive decisions, and quietly animated without becoming noisy.

## Anti-references

- Official CATAN branding, artwork, trade dress, copy, iconography, or a visually confusing imitation.
- Generic dark-mode game dashboards with neon accents and glass panels.
- Identical card grids, nested containers, ornamental gradients, and oversized marketing metrics.
- Theatrical motion that delays turns or makes repeated actions tiring.
- Mobile layouts that squeeze the full board into an unusable miniature.

## Design Principles

1. **The board is the product.** Interface chrome yields space and attention to the shared map.
2. **Rules explain themselves.** Legal targets, costs, disabled reasons, and private information are visible at the moment they matter.
3. **Motion carries meaning.** Animation shows origin, destination, ownership, and state change without blocking another decision.
4. **Warmth without nostalgia cosplay.** Natural materials and cartographic texture create atmosphere while every control remains contemporary.
5. **Trust is cumulative.** Deterministic rules, clear connection states, resilient recovery, and careful hidden-state redaction are part of the visual experience.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Player identity never depends on color alone; every seat has a crest and text label. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus, reduced motion, sound-independent feedback, readable contrast, and screen-reader announcements. Room creation and the lobby work on narrow screens; active play clearly directs users to a desktop viewport below 1024px.
